Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is a space telescope?

A space telescope is an astronomical instrument located outside Earth's atmosphere that observes celestial objects in various wavelengths of light.

2. Why are space telescopes important?

Space telescopes provide clearer images than ground-based telescopes by avoiding atmospheric distortion, allowing for more detailed observations of distant galaxies, stars, and planets.

3. How do space telescopes work?

Space telescopes use mirrors and detectors to collect and analyze light from celestial objects. They can observe in different wavelengths, including visible, infrared, and ultraviolet light.

4. What are some famous space telescopes?

Some well-known space telescopes include the Hubble Space Telescope, the James Webb Space Telescope, and the Chandra X-ray Observatory.
